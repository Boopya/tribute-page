Srinivasa Ramanujan

The Man Who Knew Infinity
Indian mathematician Srinivasa Ramanujan

Timeline of Ramanujan's Life:

  • Dec 22, 1897: First encounter with formal mathematics. He was also given a book on trigonometry.
  • Dec 22, 1899: He finally studied and work out equations to the point of knowing most of trigonometry perfectly.
  • Nov 26, 1904: Started his own research on Bernoulli numbers and Euler-Mascheroni constant.
  • Dec 26, 1905: Received a college scholarship to study at Kumbakonam, a government college.
  • Aug 25, 1907: Lost hist scholarship a couple years later, due to lack of effort into his other classes.
  • Aug 20, 1912: Started sending samples of his theorems to the University of Cambridge.
  • Sep 27, 1904: Cambridge was interested in his work and flew him out there, where he became a Fellow of the Royal Society.
  • Apr 26, 1920: Died of malnutrition and possible liver damage.
  • Jan 9, 1997: Later down the road, a while after Ramanujan's death, the movie Good Will Hunting came out, taking on the look of Ramanujan-Hardy's relationship and work.
